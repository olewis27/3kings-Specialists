Conference Program

 

 “Consider the Source”: An Experienced Clinician’s Guide to Differential Diagnosis of UE Pain
Date: January 20-21, 2017
Location: Premier Physical Therapy
Contact Hours: 8.5
*Limited to 18 people
Cost: $215 ($193.50 if you have attended a previous course)
Determining the source of arm pain and apparent nerve related symptoms in the UE can be difficult. This course will provide the participants examination and treatment of UE pain and symptoms including screening of the cervical spine, brachial plexus, thoracic outlet and peripheral nerve entrapments. A detailed review of neuroanatomy as well as examination procedures of the cervical spine and UE will be provided in order to assist the practitioner in ruling in or out nerve related cause including cervical radicular and referred pain patterns. Although a strong didactic portion will be covered, this course will provide ample lab time so each participant can learn and perform clinical tests as well as treatment options including manual therapy techniques and kinematic taping for the relief of nerve related symptoms involving the UE.

Upon completion of the seminar participants will be able to:

  1. Better understand neuroanatomy as it relates to UE pain and symptoms
  2. Identify the cervical spine as a potential source of UE pain and symptoms
  3. Perform examination techniques for the brachial plexus, TOS and peripheral nerve entrapments
  4. Demonstrate manual therapy and elastic taping techniques for the treatment of nerve related symptoms in the UE
